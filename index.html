About

I'm a junior at Johns Hopkins University majoring in Applied Mathematics and Statistics with a minor in Computer Science. I work with Prof. Daniel Khashabi in the Intelligence Amplification Lab at the Center for Language and Speech Processing. I'm currently also interning with Prof. Alessandro Zanette.

My research is in natural language processing and machine learning, with a focus on understanding and improving the reasoning behavior of large language models — including self-correction, confidence calibration, efficient inference, and creativity. I'm broadly interested in how to make small models reason like big ones and how to make big models reason more reliably.

I was a 2025 honorable mention for the CRA Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Award.

Research Interests

I'm broadly interested in NLP, machine learning, and deep learning. More specifically:

  • Improving LLM reasoning, particularly self-correction and incorporation of external feedback.
  • LLM confidence calibration, hidden-state probing, and steering for more reliable reasoning.
  • Efficient reasoning and inference-time scaling — when, and how much, to think.
  • Evaluating and improving LLM creativity, and using LLMs as judges.
  • Mathematical reasoning, robustness under question perturbation, and weak-to-strong generalization.
  • LLM agents in open-ended environments.
